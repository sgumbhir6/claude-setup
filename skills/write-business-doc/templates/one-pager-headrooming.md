# One-Pager: Headrooming

## Stakeholders
Engineers and Product Managers

## Tone
Accessible to both technical and non-technical readers. Frame solutions in terms of impact and trade-offs, not implementation details. Be concise and direct.

## Sections

### Context
What are we trying to solve for? What is the problem or opportunity, why does it matter, what prompted this exploration.

**Source:** Read `problem-statement.md` for the human-readable framing. Read `agent-context.md` if technical constraints are relevant to understanding the problem space.

### Explored Solutions
Solutions that were tried, explored, or considered. Each solution should be a sub-heading with details about the approach, what was learned, and why it was or wasn't sufficient.

**Source:** Read `findings.md` for evidence from each explored approach. Organize by solution, not by investigation order.

### Recommended Solution
Which solution is recommended and why. Should clearly connect back to the explored solutions — why this one over the others.

**Source:** Read `findings.md` for evidence supporting the recommendation. The user may also provide additional context for why one solution is preferred.
